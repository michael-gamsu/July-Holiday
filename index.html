Why this route works

  • Free London bedsaves R7,000–10,000 vs booking a hotel, bringing the whole trip to R21–30k per person.
  • Direct flightsboth ways — JNB to Heathrow direct with BA or Virgin, then London to Split direct with easyJet or Wizz Air. No layovers.

London guide

4 nights · Fri 4 – Mon 7 July · Free accommodation

Free things — and London has the world's best

British Museum Free
8 million artefacts including the Rosetta Stone and Elgin Marbles. Book a timed entry online — it fills up in summer.
Tate Modern Free
Picasso, Pollock, Warhol in a converted Bankside power station. The Turbine Hall alone is worth the visit.
Natural History Museum Free
Dinosaurs, space, wildlife — world-class. Walk with the V&A and Science Museum next door for a full South Kensington day.
National Gallery Free
Da Vinci, Turner, Van Gogh, Monet. One of the world's finest collections. On Trafalgar Square.
Southbank Walk Free
Walk from Tower Bridge to the London Eye along the Thames. Street food stalls, buskers, the Globe Theatre, and the best skyline views in London.
Changing of the Guard Free
Buckingham Palace — daily in July. Arrive 30 minutes early. Also happens at St James's Palace (less crowded).
Hyde Park Free
340 acres. Deckchairs on the Serpentine in July. Speakers' Corner is wild on Sundays. Connects to Kensington Gardens.
Borough Market Free entry
London's oldest food market near London Bridge. Graze for lunch for under £10. Busiest on Saturdays — go early.

Worth paying for

Tower of London ~£30pp
Crown Jewels, Beefeaters, 1,000 years of history. Book tickets online in advance — sells out in July.
West End show £25–90pp
Hamilton, The Lion King, Phantom. Budget tickets via TodayTix app or day-of lottery. Check TKTS booth in Leicester Square for same-day discounts.
The Shard view ~£32pp
Best skyline views in London. Book dusk slot (cheaper than peak) — the light is incredible at 8–9pm in July.
Windsor Castle day trip £28 entry + ~£20 return train
40 min from Paddington or Waterloo. Castle, St George's Chapel, Long Walk. A brilliant full day out of the city.

Getting around

  • Oyster / contactlessTap in and out everywhere. A daily cap applies so you never overpay. Zones 1–2 cap is ~£8.50. Use your SA bank card directly — contactless works exactly like Oyster.
  • Heathrow into LondonTake the Elizabeth Line (Tube) — ~£6, about 45 min to central London. Avoid the Heathrow Express (~£25) unless time is critical.
  • Bus 11Unofficial free sightseeing tour. Runs past St Paul's Cathedral, Fleet Street, Trafalgar Square, Houses of Parliament, Westminster Abbey. £1.75 flat fare.
  • WalkCentral London is very compact. Tower Bridge to Tate Modern is 10 min on foot. Hyde Park to the Natural History Museum is 20 min. Walk first, tube second.
  • Gatwick to LondonFor your London–Split flight, get to Gatwick via Thameslink from King's Cross or St Pancras — ~£10–17, avoids the expensive Gatwick Express.

Eating on a budget

  • Eat at home where possibleSince accommodation is free, cooking some meals saves significantly. A Tesco or Sainsbury's shop is straightforward and cheap.
  • Pub lunchMost pubs serve hearty food (pies, fish & chips, burgers) for £10–14. Far better value than sit-down restaurants and very atmospheric.
  • Street food marketsBorough Market, Maltby Street, Deptford Market Yard — brilliant food for £5–10. July weather means eating outside is ideal.
  • Pret a Manger / M&S Simply FoodFor breakfast and grab-and-go lunches. Fresh sandwiches and salads for £4–6. Far better than tourist cafés near landmarks.
  • Avoid restaurant rows near landmarksAnywhere on the South Bank facing the Thames, around Covent Garden piazza, or near Tower Bridge doubles prices. Walk two streets back.

Croatia guide

12 nights · Tue 8 – Sun 20 July · Split base + islands

Split — your Dalmatian base

Diocletian's Palace Free
A Roman emperor's retirement palace that became an entire city. Wander the cellars, peristyle square, and ancient alleyways — people live and work inside.
Marjan Hill Free
Forested peninsula above the city. Walk up for panoramic views of Split and the islands. Best at sunset. 30 min from Old Town on foot.
Bačvice Beach Free
The city beach right below the Old Town. Famous for picigin — a local water sport. Shallow, sandy (unusual for Croatia), and lively.
Kasjuni Beach Free
Locals' favourite — pebble beach on the western side of Marjan Hill. Clearer water, less crowded than Bačvice. 20 min walk from Old Town.
The Green Market Free
Daily market just outside the Palace walls. Fresh local produce, fish, olive oil and cheese. The best place to stock a picnic and meet locals.
The Riva Promenade Free
Split's waterfront boulevard. The place to watch boats, drink coffee, and do absolutely nothing. Magical at sunset and late at night.

Day trips from Split

Trogir Bus ~€2 · 30 min
UNESCO-listed medieval town on an island connected by bridges. Free to wander. Cathedral has an entry fee (~€3). Easy half-day.
Krka National Park ~€7 entry · ~2h bus
Cascading waterfalls and swimming holes surrounded by forest. A full day — go early to beat crowds. One of Croatia's best natural sights.
Omiš Bus ~€3 · 40 min
Pirate town at the mouth of the Cetina River canyon. Beach, fortress ruins, and river rafting available. Dramatic scenery.
Hvar (day trip) Catamaran ~€12 · 1h
Croatia's glamour island — go for the day. Beautiful Venetian town centre and fortress views. Skip overnight stays (expensive). Last catamaran back ~10pm.

Brač Island — Zlatni Rat weekend

  • Getting thereCar ferry Split → Supetar: ~50 min, ~€5 as a foot passenger. Runs frequently in July. Catamaran is faster but pricier.
  • Supetar vs BolStay in Bol on the south coast for Zlatni Rat beach. Supetar on the north is charming but further from the best beach. Bus runs between them (~40 min, ~€3).
  • Zlatni RatCroatia's most iconic beach — a pebble spit that shifts shape with tides and wind. Crystal clear water, pine shade. Get there early in July to claim a good spot.
  • WindsurfingBol is one of Europe's best windsurfing spots thanks to the maestral wind. Lessons and equipment rental from Big Blue Sport (~€50 for a session).
  • Eating in BolPlenty of good konobas serving fresh fish. Grilled sea bass or dourade with vegetables for ~€15–18pp. Wine is local and cheap — around €4 a glass.

Eating & drinking in Croatia

  • Eat at a konobaFamily-run taverns inland or on back streets — always cheaper and better than waterfront restaurants. A full meal with wine: €15–22pp.
  • Avoid the Riva terracesSitting with a sea view in Split adds 30–50% to the bill. Walk two streets back for the same food at half the price.
  • Lunch menus (ručak)Many restaurants offer a fixed-price lunch: starter, main and drink for €10–13. The best value eating in Croatia.
  • Local winePlavac Mali (red) and Pošip (white) are Croatia's best. A bottle from a local shop: €5–8. Order by the glass at konobas.
  • Market breakfastFresh bread, local cheese and tomatoes from the Green Market for €2–3. Far better than a café breakfast.

Practical Croatia tips

  • CurrencyCroatia uses the Euro (€) since Jan 2023. No currency exchange needed if coming from London.
  • FerriesBook Jadrolinija car ferries online in advance for July — foot passengers rarely need to book but car ferries sell out. Never bring a car to an island unnecessarily.
  • AccommodationAirbnb apartments in Split Old Town (10–15 min walk to Riva) offer best value. Look for places with air conditioning — July nights are warm. Book at least 3–4 months ahead.
  • Sun & heatJuly in Split: 30–33°C daily. Carry water everywhere. Avoid intense midday sun. Most activity is done morning or evening — afternoons are for the beach or a nap.
  • BeachesAlmost all Croatian beaches are pebble or rock — not sand. Water shoes help enormously. Bring or buy a pair before heading to the islands.

Money-saving moves

  • Extend London timeSince the bed is free, every extra London night costs you almost nothing. Adding a 5th night costs roughly just food + Oyster card (~R400pp/day).
  • Book Split accommodation nowJuly is peak season — apartments in Split Old Town go fast. Locking in early can save 20–30% on Airbnb vs leaving it to May/June.
  • Fly Split→London→JNB on one BA ticketBritish Airways operates Split–Heathrow–JNB as a codeshare. Booking this as one itinerary is often cheaper than two separate bookings and gives you full check-through baggage.
  • Skip Hvar overnightHvar is Croatia's most expensive island for accommodation. A day trip from Split by catamaran costs ~€12 return and lets you see all the key sights without the hotel premium.
  • Eat at lunch menus in CroatiaFixed-price lunch menus (starter + main + drink) cost €10–13pp — saving significantly vs à la carte dinner prices at the same restaurant.
  • Foot passenger only on ferriesNever take a hire car to the islands — it costs 4× more on the ferry and you don't need it. Bus networks on Brač and Hvar cover the main beaches easily.

Visas & admin

Two separate visas required — start immediately for July travel

⚠ Critical: The UK is NOT part of the Schengen Area. You need two completely separate visa applications — a UK Standard Visitor Visa AND a Schengen C Visa (which covers Croatia). Apply for both simultaneously, at least 8 weeks before travel. For July, this means starting both applications by early May at the absolute latest.

Visa timeline — what to do and when

  • Now (March)Book all flights. Get travel insurance. Start gathering bank statements and employment letter.
  • April (weeks 1–2)Book TB test at approved UK visa clinic (find list on gov.uk). Book VFS appointments for both UK and Croatian visas.
  • April (weeks 3–4)Attend TB test. Book all Croatia accommodation — needed for Schengen application. Prepare full document packs for both applications.
  • Early MaySubmit UK visa application (with TB certificate). Submit Croatian Schengen application. Both should be in no later than 8 weeks before 4 July.
  • May–JuneWait for approvals. Chase if no response after 3 weeks. Book remaining activities (West End show, Tower of London).
  • July 4Fly. Have both visa approvals, passport, insurance docs, and accommodation confirmations in your carry-on or phone.
